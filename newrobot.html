Robot Evolution & Strategy

Precision. Efficiency. Consistency. Innovation.

Robot Evolution (Owen)

  • Moved both attachment motors to the front.
    Front motors keep weight balanced and make the base easier to line up each run.
  • Reduced structural stress and instability.
    We reinforced weak points so the robot stayed rigid during turns and pushes.
  • Created a pinless gravity-based attachment system.
    Attachments drop in quickly, saving time between runs and keeping alignment consistent.
➜

Strategy & Mission Grouping (Isabella)

  • Analyzed scoring, movement, and risk.
    We compared points vs. travel distance to avoid low‑value, high‑risk paths.
  • Grouped missions by action and location.
    Missions that use the same attachment or are close together were paired.
  • Bundled nearby missions for efficient runs.
    Shorter routes reduced time and kept alignment more reliable.

Programming Improvements (Owen)

  • Transitioned from block coding to Pybricks.
    Faster code loops let us react to errors immediately.
  • Improved turn precision and speed control.
    We fine‑tuned power levels to reduce drift and overshoot.
  • Enhanced sensor-based consistency.
    Sensors corrected small errors so runs stayed repeatable.

Sensors & Consistency (Jonathan)

  • Gyro sensor for exact angles.
    The gyro keeps turns accurate even if the floor changes.
  • Motor encoders for precise distances.
    Encoders track wheel rotations to keep distances consistent.
  • Auto-corrected drift while driving.
    Small corrections keep the robot centered on its path.

Testing & Refinement (Isabella)

  • Repeated testing for data-based adjustments.
    We logged results to see patterns and fix weak points.
  • Smoothed starts and stops.
    Gentler acceleration reduced wheel slip.
  • Improved reliability during competition.
    Stable routines meant fewer resets under pressure.
